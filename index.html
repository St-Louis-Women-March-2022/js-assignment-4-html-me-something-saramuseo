Now We Are Six

My beautiful Rosemarie was born in April 2017. Three days of labor and she came out on her own time. She just turned five and I started thinking about her sixth birthday and this poem. My aunt gave me A.A. Milne's book when I turned six, and my partner and I will do the same for Rosemarie. She continues to grow and develop her own personality, as we all do beyond the age of six. I love her empathy, her curiosity, her bravery, her independence. She is my love bug, and I dedicate his page to her.

To my Rosemarie
- Love, your Mommy.

"When I was One, I had just begun."

"When I was Two, I was nearly new."

"When I was Three I was hardly me."

"When I was Four, I was not much more."

"When I was Five, I was just alive."

"But now I am Six, I'm as clever as clever,
So I think I'll be six now for ever and ever."

Source: Poem by A.A. Milne, from Family Friends website.
